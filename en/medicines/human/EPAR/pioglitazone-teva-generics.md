## Overview

On 26 January 2012, Teva Generics B.V. officially notified the Committee for Medicinal Products for Human Use (CHMP) that it wishes to withdraw its application for a marketing authorisation for Pioglitazone Teva Generics, for the treatment of type-2 diabetes.

## What is Pioglitazone Teva Generics?

Pioglitazone Teva Generics is a medicine that contains the active substance pioglitazone. It was to be available as tablets (15, 30 and 45 mg).

Pioglitazone Teva Generics was developed as a 'generic medicine'. This means that Pioglitazone Teva Generics is similar to a 'reference medicine' already authorised in the European Union (EU) called Actos.

## What was Pioglitazone Teva Generics expected to be used for?

Pioglitazone Teva Generics was to be used to treat type- diabetes in adults, particularly those who are overweight. It was to be used in addition to diet and exercise.

Pioglitazone Teva Generics was to used on its own in patients for whom metformin (another antidiabetes medicine) is not suitable.

Pioglitazone Teva Generics was also to be used in combination with metformin in patients who are not satisfactorily controlled on metformin alone.

Pioglitazone Teva Generics was also to be used together with insulin in patients who are not satisfactorily controlled with insulin alone and cannot take metformin.

## How is Pioglitazone Teva Generics expected to work?

Type 2 diabetes is a disease in which the pancreas does not make enough insulin to control the level of glucose in the blood or when the body is unable to use insulin effectively. The active substance in Pioglitazone Teva Generics, pioglitazone, makes cells (fat, muscle and liver) more sensitive to insulin, which means that the body makes better use of the insulin it produces. As a consequence, the blood glucose levels are reduced and this helps to control type 2 diabetes.

## What did the company present to support its application?

Because Pioglitazone Teva Generics is a generic medicine, studies in patients have been limited to tests to determine that it is bioequivalent to the reference medicine, Actos. Two medicines are bioequivalent when they produce the same levels of the active substance in the body.

## How far into the evaluation was the application when it was withdrawn?

The evaluation had finished and the CHMP had given a positive opinion. The company withdrew before the European Commission had issued a decision on this opinion.

## What was the recommendation of the CHMP at that time?

The CHMP concluded that, in accordance with EU requirements, Pioglitazone Teva Generics had been shown to have comparable quality and to be bioequivalent to Actos. Therefore, at the time of the withdrawal, the CHMP had given a positive opinion, recommending that a marketing authorisation be granted for Pioglitazone Teva Generics for the treatment of type 2 diabetes.

## Key facts

Name of medicine Pioglitazone Teva Generics Active substance pioglitazone hydrochloride International non-proprietary name (INN) or common name pioglitazone Therapeutic area (MeSH) Diabetes Mellitus, Type 2 Anatomical therapeutic chemical (ATC) code A10BG03 EMA product number EMEA/H/C/002448

Generic

This is a generic medicine, which is developed to be the same as a medicine that has already been authorised, called the reference medicine. A generic medicine contains the same active substance(s) as the reference medicine, and is used at the same dose(s) to treat the same disease(s).
