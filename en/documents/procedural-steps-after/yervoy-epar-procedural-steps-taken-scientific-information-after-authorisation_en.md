In addition, the MAH took the opportunity to implement editorial   |            | Myasthenia Gravis Overlap Syndrome (presenting as an overlap of either two or all three conditions), some with fatal outcome, have been reported. Early recognition and aggressive management are essential to address associated morbidity and risk of mortality. For Grade 3 or 4 Myocarditis-Myositis- Myasthenia Gravis Overlap Syndrome, ipilimumab or nivolumab or their combination must be permanently discontinued (see SmPC section 4.2). Corticosteroids should be initiated as clinically indicated. For Grade 2 Myocarditis-Myositis- Myasthenia Gravis Overlap Syndrome, ipilimumab or nivolumab or their combination should be withheld and corticosteroids initiated as clinically indicated (see section 4.2). Upon improvement, resumption of nivolumab may be considered after corticosteroid taper. If worsening or no improvement occurs despite initiation of corticosteroids, corticosteroid dose should be adjusted as clinically indicated, and ipilimumab or nivolumab or their combination must be permanently discontinued. For more information, please refer to the Summary of Product Characteristics.   |
